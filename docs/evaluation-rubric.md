# Evaluation Rubric

This rubric explains how Intelligent Brain Company should evaluate an idea and how humans should judge result quality.

## Product Verdict Scale

- Go: strong demand signal, feasible MVP path, acceptable complexity, and credible monetization.
- Maybe: promising but blocked by unresolved assumptions, tighter scope requirements, or weaker proof of demand.
- No-Go: too much execution risk, weak demand signal, or poor business viability for the current constraints.

## Five Scorecard Dimensions

### Market Demand

Question: is there a clear user segment with a painful enough problem?

- 1 to 3: vague audience, weak urgency, poor wedge market
- 4 to 6: some demand signal but weak differentiation or unclear purchase trigger
- 7 to 8: clear target segment and believable need
- 9 to 10: urgent problem, strong wedge, obvious adoption path

### Technical Feasibility

Question: can the team build a first useful version with reasonable engineering effort?

- 1 to 3: major technical unknowns or heavy dependency risk
- 4 to 6: feasible but with meaningful integration or execution risk
- 7 to 8: practical MVP path with manageable complexity
- 9 to 10: straightforward implementation and low technical uncertainty

### Execution Complexity

Question: how hard is it to coordinate product, engineering, operations, and go-to-market?

- 1 to 3: low operational complexity
- 4 to 6: moderate cross-functional load
- 7 to 8: high coordination burden
- 9 to 10: very difficult to execute under current constraints

Note: in the UI, lower complexity is better even though the scale is displayed as a score.

### Time To MVP

Question: how realistic is a fast MVP launch?

- 1 to 3: slow path, many prerequisites
- 4 to 6: possible but likely delayed by dependencies
- 7 to 8: credible short-cycle MVP path
- 9 to 10: unusually fast route to a first testable product

### Monetization Potential

Question: is there a believable path from value to revenue?

- 1 to 3: unclear willingness to pay
- 4 to 6: possible revenue model but weak proof
- 7 to 8: strong monetization logic with a clear buyer
- 9 to 10: highly credible buyer, pricing, and repeatable revenue path

## Workflow Quality Criteria

Use these criteria to evaluate the overall system, not only the idea:

1. Completeness: did the output cover research, departments, and board decision?
2. Internal consistency: do department recommendations align with each other?
3. Actionability: could a founder take the next step from this result?
4. Traceability: can a reviewer see what changed after intervention?
5. Distinct role behavior: do departments sound meaningfully different?

## Recommended Human Review Process

1. Run the same idea at least twice.
2. Check whether the high-level verdict is stable.
3. Insert one intervention at research or board stage.
4. Confirm that the new version reflects the intervention in the scorecard and markdown diff.
5. Compare against a direct prompt baseline.
